Biogas, A Renewable Energy Source

Biogas is a renewable energy source produced by the breakdown of organic matter, like food waste or manure, by microorganisms in the absence of oxygen. It primarily contains methane and carbon dioxide and can be used for heating, electricity, or as vehicle fuel.

  • Why Biogas Needs to Maintain Temperature? Biogas production relies on maintaining optimal temperatures (35-40°C) to keep microorganisms active, ensure process stability, maximize methane production, and prevent inefficiencies or system failure. Steady temperature is key to efficient and sustainable biogas generation.
